<p><p>The cardiovascular pericardial patch market comprises three main types: autologous, xenograft, and synthetic patches. Autologous patches are derived from the patient's own tissue, reducing the risk of rejection and complications. Xenograft patches are sourced from animal tissues, providing a viable alternative when autologous options are unavailable. Synthetic patches are made from biocompatible materials, offering durability and versatility in surgical applications. Each type serves unique needs in cardiac surgery and repair, influencing choice based on patient-specific conditions and surgical requirements.</p></p>
