<p><p>The antimicrobial catheters market primarily includes two types: antibiotic-coated catheters and silver-coated catheters. Antibiotic-coated catheters are designed with a surface that releases antibiotics to prevent bacterial colonization and infections associated with catheter use. Silver-coated catheters, on the other hand, utilize silver ions known for their antimicrobial properties to reduce the risk of infection and biofilm formation. Both types aim to enhance patient safety, reduce healthcare costs, and improve outcomes by minimizing catheter-associated infections.</p></p>
