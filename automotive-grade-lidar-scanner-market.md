<p><p>The automotive grade LiDAR scanner market is primarily divided into two types: Mechanical LiDAR and Solid-State LiDAR. Mechanical LiDAR utilizes rotating components to scan the environment, offering wide coverage and high-resolution mapping, making it suitable for advanced driver-assistance systems (ADAS). In contrast, Solid-State LiDAR is characterized by a more compact design with no moving parts, enhancing durability and reliability while significantly reducing production costs. Both technologies are crucial for enabling autonomous driving and enhancing vehicle safety.</p></p>
