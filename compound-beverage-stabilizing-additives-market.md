<p><p>The compound beverage stabilizing additives market includes various types that enhance texture, prevent separation, and improve shelf life. Gum Arabic is a natural gum used for its emulsifying properties. Carboxymethyl Cellulose is a thickener and stabilizer that also improves mouthfeel. Xanthan Gum provides viscosity and stabilizes emulsions. Carrageenan, derived from seaweed, is used for gelling and thickening. Pectin, a plant-based polysaccharide, acts as a gelling agent. Other additives may include modified starches and locust bean gum, further broadening stabilization options.</p></p>
